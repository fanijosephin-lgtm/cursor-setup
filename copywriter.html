Frequently asked questions about Copywriter

We already have writers. What's the point?
Copywriter makes your writers faster and more consistent. You're not replacing your team — you're upgrading the system they run on. Structured briefs, Knowledge Base grounding, and clear optimization guidance mean your writers deliver better first drafts with fewer revision cycles.
How do you prevent hallucinations?
By grounding drafts in your client's Knowledge Base and requiring real inputs for claims, policies, and differentiators. You get content aligned to what's true for that business — not generic AI guesses.
How is this different from a content optimization tool?
Most content tools only analyze Google SERPs and give you a score. Copywriter builds briefs from SERPs and AI citations, drafts from a client Knowledge Base, then guides optimization and uniqueness. It's a complete agency workflow — not just an editor with a score.
Does this work across different niches?
Yes. Whether your clients are in SaaS, local services, ecommerce, healthcare, or any other vertical, Copywriter adapts because it pulls real competitive data and uses your client's specific Knowledge Base. The workflow is niche-agnostic.
What does the uniqueness step actually do?
It provides a dedicated step to inject proprietary insights, unique angles, proof, experience, and differentiators — content elements competitors can't copy. This is how you earn information gain and stand out in both search and AI answers.
How do SERPs and AI competitor sources get used in the brief?
We analyze the top-ranking pages in Google and the sources that AI platforms like ChatGPT surface and cite. Both sets of competitors inform your brief's topics, subtopics, entities, and coverage requirements — giving you the full picture of what you need to cover.
What is Optimize mode?
Optimize mode is a workflow inside Copywriter for improving existing published content. Paste a URL, and Rankability fetches the live page, runs a fresh SERP + AI analysis, and shows you exactly what to add, cut, or rewrite to close the gap between your current content and what's ranking. It's ideal for recovering traffic or refreshing underperforming pages.
