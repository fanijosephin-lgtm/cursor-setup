# Overall Research Notes

Across the selected videos, Matt Diggity frames AI-powered SEO as a combination of content structure, trust building, distribution, community participation, and external validation. The most useful recent videos focus less on simply generating content with AI and more on making content and brands visible in the sources AI systems appear to trust.

Several themes repeat: AI search visibility depends on trusted third-party signals, Reddit and editorial coverage can influence discovery, existing content should be repurposed for new search behaviors, and content needs to be direct, structured, and easy for AI systems to interpret. No verified full transcripts were available from the public YouTube RSS feed, so the summaries should be treated as metadata- and description-based research rather than transcript analysis.

# Key Takeaways for This Repository

* Use AI to repurpose verified content into platform-specific assets, not only to create new articles.
* Build AI search workflows around trust signals such as digital PR, third-party mentions, Reddit visibility, and backlinks.
* Structure content with clear headings, direct answers, and precise claims.
* Track search visibility beyond Google, including AI referrals, Reddit traffic, and audience discovery platforms.
* Avoid treating AI SEO as only on-page optimization; include distribution, community, and authority-building steps.
