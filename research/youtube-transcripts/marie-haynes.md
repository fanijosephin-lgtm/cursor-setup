# Overall Research Notes

Across these videos, Marie Haynes treats AI Search as the next phase of Google Search rather than a separate discipline. Her YouTube work connects AI Overviews, AI Mode, Search Console reporting, Google I/O announcements, agentic search, E-E-A-T, YMYL content, and core updates to a broader question: how can sites become genuinely useful, trusted, and technically understandable to both people and AI systems?

Several themes repeat across the selected videos: AI Search measurement is becoming more important; AI visibility should be tied to user trust and source preference; non-commodity content matters more as AI systems summarize common information; and technical SEO is expanding to include agent-readiness, accessibility, structured knowledge, and AI-readable content formats. Complete transcripts were not publicly verified, so the summaries above avoid quotation-level claims and stay tied to public metadata and descriptions.

# Key Takeaways for This Repository

* Use AI Search reporting from Google Search Console where available, but pair it with ordinary Search data and business outcomes.
* Prioritize non-commodity content, first-hand experience, expert review, and clear source trust over scaled generic publishing.
* Treat AI Mode, AI Overviews, preferred sources, and agentic search as connected parts of Google's evolving Search system.
* Build E-E-A-T and YMYL review into AI-assisted content workflows before publication.
* Add technical checks for AI and agent accessibility alongside traditional crawlability, indexing, performance, and structured data.
