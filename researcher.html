Frequently asked questions about Researcher

Where do Discover keywords come from?
Google, People Also Ask (PAA), YouTube, Reddit, trending topics, and AI expansion — plus optional Knowledge Base and Google Search Console enrichment for highly personalized, data-driven keyword suggestions.
Can I use it for local markets?
Yes. Choose your target country and optionally set a specific location (e.g., "Austin, TX" or "London, UK") for localized research with location-specific SERP data and content recommendations.
How fast is it?
Discover runs a multi-step pipeline pulling from multiple sources simultaneously and typically completes in a few minutes. Analyze (bulk keyword metrics) is near-instant.
Can I export keyword data?
Yes. Copy to clipboard, CSV, and Google Sheets export options are all available. You can also save keywords to named lists within the platform and share them with clients for approval.
Can I share keyword research with clients for approval?
Yes. Select the keywords you want approved and generate a shareable link. Your client receives a branded page showing every keyword with metrics. They can approve, reject, or leave comments on individual keywords. Share links support white-label branding and expiration controls.
How do we turn research into content?
Add keywords to lists, export to your team, or use one-click handoff to open keywords directly in content creation. The Keyword Report for any term shows opportunity score, metrics, SERP analysis, PAA, related searches, and AI presence data.
What is the KO Score?
The KO Score (Keyword Overview Score) is a proprietary weighted score combining search volume, keyword difficulty, CPC value, and search intent to rank keyword opportunities. A higher KO Score means the keyword is more strategically valuable.
