Frequently asked questions

How do I know if I'm ranking in Google AI Overviews?

Add your target keywords to Tracker and run a scan. Tracker detects whether an AI Overview appears for each keyword and tells you if your brand or website is cited as a source. No manual checking needed.

What's the best AI Overviews tracker?

The best AI Overview tracker should detect AIO presence automatically, capture all cited source URLs, track your position among sources, and monitor changes over time. Rankability's Tracker does all of this, plus tracks five other AI search platforms from the same dashboard.

What's the best software for tracking keyword rankings in AI Overviews?

Tracker tracks your keyword rankings across both AI Overviews and traditional organic results. Each scan captures whether an AIO appears, which sources are cited, and where your brand falls in the citation order. All data is tracked historically so you can measure trends.

How do I track AI Overviews rankings over time?

Tracker scans your keywords on a schedule you set — daily, weekly, or custom. Each scan records the full AI Overview state: whether it appeared, which sources were cited, your position, and competitor presence. View trend lines over weeks and months to understand how your AIO visibility is changing.

What's the difference between AI Overviews and AI Mode?

AI Overviews are the AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of traditional Google search results. AI Mode is Google's newer conversational search interface that provides deeper, multi-turn AI responses. Tracker is designed to track your visibility across both formats.

Can I see which of my pages Google AI Overviews cites?

Yes. Tracker captures the exact source URLs that Google references in each AI Overview. You can see which pages on your domain earn citations, which don't, and how this changes over time.

Does Rankability only track Google AI Overviews?

No. Tracker tracks brand visibility across Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google Gemini, Brave Search, and traditional Google organic results — all from one dashboard with unified reporting.
