§ 01 · The shift

Your buyers are choosing from answers, not search results.

When prospects ask AI tools who to trust, compare, or buy from, they may never reach your website. Traditional SEO still matters, but ranking is no longer the whole game.

The new question is: does AI understand your brand well enough to recommend it?

ChatGPT answer · sample

Prompt: "best [category] tools 2026"

The top tools in 2026 are Competitor A, Competitor B, and Competitor C. They're cited across G2, Reddit, and industry roundups for ease of use, integrations, and pricing transparency...

Your brand: not mentioned in 84 of 142 priority prompts.

01

Invisible in AI answers

Your category gets queried hundreds of times a day inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude. But your brand isn't in the response, and buyers move on before they ever hear your name.

02

Competitors cited instead of you

Answer engines lean on a small set of trusted sources. When your competitors are referenced and you aren't, AI defaults to recommending them, even when your offering is stronger.

03

No clear way to measure AI visibility

Without prompt-level tracking, you can't tell what AI says about you, what sources it relies on, or whether your work this quarter actually moved the answers.

§ 07 · Questions

The honest answers, up front.

If your question isn't here, bring it to the discovery call.

01 What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of making your brand easier for AI systems to retrieve, cite, and recommend. It covers how ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Google AI Overviews understand your category, brand, products, and competitors, and what it takes to influence the answers they generate.

02 How is AEO different from SEO and GEO?

SEO optimizes for traditional search engines and ranking pages. GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) is one slice of AEO focused on generative answers. AEO is the broader strategy: measurement, entity authority, citations, retrievability, and the technical foundation that influences every AI surface, not just the ranked page.

03 Can we optimize existing content first?

Yes. Most engagements start by auditing and restructuring high-value pages already attracting traffic, before producing new content. The fastest wins typically come from improving entity clarity, internal linking, schema, and retrievability on pages your audience already reads.

04 How do you measure AI visibility?

We track a defined set of high-intent prompts across the major answer engines and score brand mention rate, citation share, answer inclusion, source coverage, and competitor share of voice. Results are delivered as a diagnostic with prioritized next steps, not a dashboard you have to interpret yourself.

05 How long does it take to see results?

Audit findings are typically delivered within 2–4 weeks. Visibility improvements depend on the change required: schema, on-page, and entity fixes can move within weeks, while authority and citation-side work compounds over months.

06 Do we need developer support?

Most recommendations are executable by an SEO or web team. Some technical changes (schema, internal linking, CMS-level templates) benefit from a developer, but every recommendation is sized for who needs to touch it and how long it should take.

07 Will this help traditional SEO too?

Yes. Better entity clarity, internal linking, technical SEO, schema, and authority signals improve both traditional rankings and AI retrievability. AEO is not a replacement for SEO. It is the next layer.
